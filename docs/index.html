Real Questions, Real Answers

See how The Dental Angel explains dental care.

"Why does my dentist want to do a root canal? Can't they just fill it?"

"That's a really common question! Here's the difference: A filling repairs damage on the outside of your tooth. But when decay or infection reaches the nerve inside the tooth — that's when a root canal becomes necessary. Think of it like this: if your house has a leaky roof, you patch it. But if termites get into the walls, you need to treat what's inside..."

The Dental Angel
"My treatment plan says I need 'scaling and root planing.' What is that?"

"Ah, scaling and root planing — sometimes called a 'deep cleaning.' It's basically a more thorough cleaning that goes below the gum line. Regular cleanings polish the visible parts of your teeth. This one cleans the roots where bacteria like to hide. It's typically recommended when there are signs of gum disease..."
