# The Dental Angel - Design Specification
## UI/UX Design System | Clean Vibe | January 2026

> Generated by IdeaRalph | PMF Score: 9.8/10
> Target: 45-65 year old patients seeking clarity on dental treatment plans

---

# 1. TARGET AUDIENCE ANALYSIS

> Based on the PRD targeting **45-65 year old patients** facing expensive dental procedures, who value **trust, clarity, and family involvement**, and fear being taken advantage of or making wrong decisions.

**Primary Persona: "Concerned Carol"**
- Age: 45-65, predominantly female (60%)
- Tech comfort: Moderate (uses Facebook, texts, basic apps)
- Values: Family, health, financial responsibility
- Fears: Being taken advantage of, making wrong health decisions
- Context: Just left dentist with $1,000-$50,000 treatment plan, confused and uncertain

**Key Design Implications:**
- **Large, readable text** (minimum 16px body, 18px+ preferred)
- **High contrast** (WCAG AAA where possible)
- **Simple navigation** (minimal cognitive load)
- **Warm, calming colors** (not clinical/sterile)
- **Obvious CTAs** (no guessing what to tap)
- **Generous spacing** (not cramped, easy to tap)
- **Human warmth** (never feels robotic)

# 3. DR. ANGEL PERSONA DESIGN

## 3.1 Visual Appearance

**Dr. Angel Avatar Characteristics:**
- **Age appearance**: 55-65 (experienced but approachable)
- **Gender**: Non-specific/androgynous OR offer male/female options
- **Expression**: Warm smile, kind eyes, approachable
- **Attire**: Professional but not intimidating (polo shirt or casual button-down, no white coat)
- **Style**: Slightly stylized/illustrated (not hyperrealistic - avoids uncanny valley)

**Recommended AI Avatar Tools:**
| Tool | Pros | Cons |
|------|------|------|
| **HeyGen** | Professional, customizable, good lip sync | $$ |
| **D-ID** | Good quality, API available | Less customization |
| **Synthesia** | Many avatar options | Can look generic |

**Recommendation**: HeyGen for best quality + customization

## 3.2 Voice Characteristics

**Dr. Angel Voice Profile:**
- **Tone**: Warm, friendly, reassuring
- **Pace**: Slightly slower than average (easy to follow)
- **Accent**: Neutral American (broadly accessible)
- **Personality**: Like a kind uncle/aunt explaining something
- **No**: Medical jargon, condescension, rushed delivery

**ElevenLabs Voice Settings:**
```
Stability: 0.65 (some variation, not robotic)
Similarity: 0.80 (consistent but natural)
Style: 0.45 (conversational, not dramatic)
Speaker Boost: ON (clarity for older users)
```

## 3.3 Personality in Text

**Writing Style:**
- Uses contractions ("you'll" not "you will")
- Addresses user directly ("Let me explain...")
- Uses analogies ("Think of a crown like a helmet for your tooth")
- Acknowledges emotions ("I know this can feel overwhelming")
- Occasional gentle humor ("The famous root canal! It's got quite the reputation...")

**Sample Greetings:**
- "Hi there! I'm Dr. Angel. What's on your mind about your dental care today?"
- "Welcome back! Ready to tackle some more questions together?"
- "Got your treatment plan? Let's go through it together."

## 5.1 Font Selection

**Primary Font**: **Inter**
- Why: Highly readable, designed for screens, excellent at all sizes

# 9. MOTION & ANIMATION

## 9.1 Principles

- **Subtle**: Animations enhance, never distract
- **Fast**: 200-300ms for most transitions
- **Purposeful**: Every animation communicates something
- **Accessible**: Respect reduced motion preferences

## 9.2 Key Animations

| Element | Animation | Duration | Easing |
|---------|-----------|----------|--------|

# 13. DESIGN DO'S AND DON'TS

### DO ✓
- Use generous whitespace
- Make buttons obviously clickable
- Use plain language everywhere
- Show loading states clearly
- Provide clear feedback for actions
- Make the Dr. Angel avatar prominent
- Use calming, soft colors
- Include disclaimers prominently
- Make touch targets large (48px+)
- Support font scaling

### DON'T ✗
- Use medical/clinical imagery
- Make text smaller than 16px
- Hide important actions
- Use jargon in UI copy
- Make users guess what to do
- Overwhelm with options
- Use harsh/bright colors
- Make it feel like a medical app
- Use small, hard-to-tap buttons
- Ignore accessibility
